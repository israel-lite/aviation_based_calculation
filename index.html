Speed in Aviation

What is speed in aviation?

In aviation, speed is measured in knots. One knot equals one nautical mile per hour.

1 knot = 1 NM per hour

Example: If an aircraft flies at 120 knots, it travels 120 nautical miles in one hour.

Nautical Mile

What is a nautical mile?

A nautical mile (NM) is based on the circumference of the Earth and is used for air and sea navigation.

1 NM = 1.852 km
1 NM = 1.151 miles

It's approximately one minute of latitude on Earth.

Knots vs km/h

Conversion between units:

1 knot = 1.852 km/h
1 km/h = 0.540 knots

Most countries use km/h for ground vehicles, but aviation universally uses knots.

TSD Formulas

The three fundamental formulas:

Distance = Speed × Time
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Remember: If you know any two values, you can always find the third!
